How We Chose These Filters

Our selection criteria focused on four factors:

  1. Verified MERV rating — Not all filters labeled "MERV 12" actually test to that standard. We prioritized brands with independent testing.
  2. Airflow balance — A filter that catches everything but chokes your system isn't a good filter.
  3. Size availability — A great filter that only comes in three sizes isn't useful to most people. Check our size guide for help.
  4. Value over time — We calculated cost per month of use, not just sticker price.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MERV rating is best for home use?

MERV 11 or 12 is the sweet spot for most homes. It captures dust, pollen, pet dander, and fine particles without restricting airflow on modern HVAC systems. Read our full MERV rating guide for details.

Are expensive HVAC filters worth it?

It depends on your needs. For homes with pets or allergies, upgrading from MERV 8 to MERV 12 makes a noticeable difference in air quality. The price difference is typically $3–$5 per filter.

How often should I change my HVAC filter?

Standard 1-inch pleated filters should be changed every 90 days. With pets, every 60 days. With pets and allergies, every 45 days. Deep pleat 4-inch filters last 6–12 months. See our complete maintenance guide.

Can I use a MERV 13 filter in my furnace?

Most systems built in the last 15–20 years with variable-speed blowers can handle MERV 13. Older single-speed systems may struggle with the increased airflow resistance. Check your HVAC manual or consult a technician.

What size HVAC filter do I need?

Check the label on your current filter — the size is printed on the frame. If there's no label, measure the length, width, and depth of the filter slot. Common sizes include 16×25×1, 20×25×1, and 20×20×1. Our filter size guide has the full breakdown.
