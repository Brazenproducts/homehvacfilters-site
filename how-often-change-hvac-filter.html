How Often Should You Change Your HVAC Filter?

Last updated April 2026 · 8 min read

The honest answer: it depends. But "it depends" isn't helpful when you're standing in front of your return vent wondering if that filter has been in there for two months or six. So here's a practical breakdown.

The simplest rule: Standard 1-inch pleated filter → every 90 days. Pets → every 60 days. Pets AND allergies → every 45 days. Set a phone reminder.

Factors That Affect Filter Life

🐾 Pets

This is the single biggest factor. Pet hair and dander load up a filter fast. One dog or cat? Knock 30 days off the standard lifespan. Multiple pets? You might be changing every 45 days. If you have a heavy shedder, check your filter monthly. Our best filter for pets pick handles dander especially well.

🤧 Allergies or Asthma

If anyone in your home has respiratory issues, err on the side of changing more frequently. A loaded filter isn't just less effective — it can actually release trapped particles back into the air. For allergy sufferers, we recommend checking monthly and changing when it looks gray. See our allergy filter guide for the best options.

🏜️ Dusty Environment

Live near a dirt road? In the desert Southwest? Near active construction? Your filter is working overtime. Dusty environments can cut filter life in half. Check monthly.

🏠 Home Size and Occupancy

More people = more dust, skin cells, and activity stirring up particles. A family of five will load a filter faster than a single person.

🔥 Season

Your HVAC runs more in extreme temperatures. In the dead of winter or peak summer, the blower runs more hours per day. You may need to change more frequently during peak seasons.

Signs Your Filter Needs Changing

  • The filter looks gray or dark. Hold it up to a light — if you can't see through it, it's done.
  • Dust buildup on vents. If dust is accumulating on supply vents faster than usual, the filter is probably saturated.
  • Increased energy bills. A clogged filter makes your system work harder.
  • Reduced airflow. Put your hand near a vent. If airflow feels weaker than normal, check the filter.
  • Musty smell. A damp, loaded filter can develop mold. Change immediately and inspect for moisture issues.
  • System short cycling. If your HVAC turns on and off more frequently, a clogged filter could be the cause.

What Happens If You Don't Change It?

  1. Reduced airflow — Energy consumption increases 5–15%.
  2. Frozen evaporator coil — Can lead to water damage when it thaws.
  3. Compressor damage — The most expensive component. A new compressor costs $1,500–$3,000+.
  4. Poor air quality — A saturated filter stops catching particles and can release them back.
  5. Shortened system life — Constant strain means earlier failure.
Real talk: A $15 filter change every 90 days is the cheapest HVAC maintenance you can do. Skipping it can lead to repairs costing hundreds or thousands. It's the oil change of home ownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I change a 1-inch pleated filter?

Every 90 days for standard homes. Every 60 days if you have pets. Every 45 days if you have pets and allergies.

How long do 4-inch HVAC filters last?

Deep pleat 4-inch and 5-inch filters typically last 6 to 12 months because they have significantly more surface area than 1-inch filters.

What happens if I don't change my HVAC filter?

A clogged filter reduces airflow, increases energy consumption by 5–15%, can freeze your evaporator coil, damage your compressor ($1,500–$3,000+ to replace), and release trapped particles back into your air.

How do I know when my filter needs changing?

Hold it up to a light — if you can't see through it, it's done. Other signs: dust buildup on vents, increased energy bills, reduced airflow, musty smell, or system short cycling.
