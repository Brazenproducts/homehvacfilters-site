HVAC Filter Size Guide — How to Find Your Filter Size

Last updated April 2026 · 7 min read

Getting the right filter size is more important than getting the right MERV rating. A filter that's too small lets unfiltered air bypass around the edges. A filter that's too big won't fit in the slot. Here's how to get it right.

Step 1: Check Your Current Filter

The easiest way to find your size: pull out your current filter and look at the frame. The nominal size is printed on the cardboard edge — something like "20×25×1" (length × width × depth in inches).

Pro tip: Write your filter size on a piece of tape and stick it to the return vent or furnace. You'll never have to measure again. Also write it in your phone notes.

Step 2: How to Measure (If No Label)

If your current filter doesn't have a label, or you don't have a filter installed:

  1. Measure the filter slot opening — length, width, and depth — with a tape measure.
  2. Round up to the nearest inch. If you measure 19.5 × 24.5 × 0.75, your nominal size is 20×25×1.
  3. Always buy by nominal size. Filters are manufactured slightly undersized to fit the slot.

Nominal vs. Actual Size

This trips people up. Here's the deal:

  • Nominal size = the rounded-up size on the label (what you buy). Example: 20×25×1.
  • Actual size = the precise physical dimensions. Example: 19.5×24.5×0.75.

Filters are intentionally made slightly smaller than nominal so they slide into the slot easily. A "20×25×1" filter is actually about 19.5×24.5×0.75 inches. This is normal and correct — the slight gap is sealed by the filter frame and housing.

Don't buy by actual size. If you measure 19.5×24.5 and search for that exact size, you'll either find nothing or get a filter that's too small. Round up and buy the nominal size.

Installation Tips

  • Check the airflow arrow. Every filter has an arrow on the frame indicating airflow direction. It should point toward the blower/furnace — in the direction air flows through the system.
  • The filter should slide in snugly but not require force. If you're pushing hard, it's the wrong size.
  • Close the access panel completely. An open panel creates a pressure imbalance.
  • Write the date on the new filter with a Sharpie so you know when it was installed.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most common HVAC filter size?

The most common residential sizes are 16×25×1, 20×25×1, and 20×20×1. These three cover the majority of standard residential systems.

What is the difference between nominal and actual filter size?

Nominal size is the rounded-up size on the label (e.g., 20×25×1). Actual size is the precise measurement, typically 1/4 to 1/2 inch smaller in each dimension (e.g., 19.5×24.5×0.75). Always buy by nominal size.

How do I measure my HVAC filter size?

Remove your current filter and check the label. If there's no label, measure the length, width, and depth of the filter slot with a tape measure, then round up to the nearest inch for the nominal size.

What if my filter size is non-standard?

Nordic Pure offers 40+ sizes including many odd dimensions. For truly custom sizes, BowTie Filters makes made-to-order filters in any dimension.
